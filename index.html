Welcome to Vietnam

One Country,
A Thousand Stories

Vietnam stretches over 1,650 km along the South China Sea, cradling a dazzling variety of landscapes — from the terraced rice fields of Sapa in the north to the white-sand beaches of Phu Quoc in the south.

Whether you are drawn to the rhythms of Hanoi's Old Quarter, the lantern-lit nights of Hoi An, or the boat markets of the Mekong Delta, every corner of this country offers an experience unlike anywhere else on Earth.

This guide is curated for travellers who want to go beyond the tourist trail — to eat where locals eat, discover hidden beaches, and stand inside temples that have witnessed a thousand years of history.

Where to Go in Vietnam

Three unmissable experiences — whether you have a week or a month.

🏖️ Beach Recommendation

Phú Quốc Island
— The Pearl Island

Vietnam's largest island sits in the Gulf of Thailand, fringed by 25 beaches of powder-white sand and water so clear you can see the coral below. The northern part of the island remains protected rainforest, home to rare wildlife and hidden waterfalls.

Best visited between November and April when skies are clear and seas are calm. Stay in the south near Long Beach for vibrant nightlife, or head north to Ong Lang for total seclusion.

Best Season Nov – Apr
Nearest City Phu Quoc Town
Must Try Grilled seafood & pepper crab

🛕 Temple Recommendation

Mỹ Sơn Sanctuary
— Cham Highlands

Tucked into a lush jungle valley near Hoi An, Mỹ Sơn is a complex of over 70 Hindu temples built by the Cham civilization between the 4th and 14th centuries. A UNESCO World Heritage Site since 1999, it is Vietnam's most important archaeological monument.

Arrive early in the morning before tour groups descend. The mist that clings to the surrounding hills at dawn makes the ruins feel like a scene from another world. Combine your visit with an afternoon in Hoi An's Ancient Town, just 40 km away.

Era 4th – 14th Century
UNESCO Since 1999
Combine with Hoi An Ancient Town

🌏 Recommended Country Route

North-to-South
— The Classic Vietnam

Fly into Hanoi, spend two nights in the Old Quarter, then take an overnight cruise through Hạ Long Bay — a seascape of 1,600 limestone islands rising from jade water. Head south to Hội An for culture and cuisine, continue to Hồ Chí Minh City for urban energy, and finish in the Mekong Delta.

This 14-day itinerary gives you the full range of Vietnam's diversity, from misty mountain terraces in Sapa to the flat, river-threaded landscape of the south. Best done between October and April when central and southern Vietnam are driest.

Duration 14 – 21 days
Best Season Oct – Apr
Start City Hanoi
